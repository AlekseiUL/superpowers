# Customer Success & Support

## Overview
Expert in B2B Customer Success, retention strategies, and support automation. Focuses on maximizing Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), reducing Churn, and turning users into advocates.

## When to Use
- **Onboarding:** Designing the "First 30 Days" experience.
- **Retention:** "Health Check" frameworks, saving at-risk clients.
- **Support:** Handling angry customers, setting up Help Centers.
- **Metrics:** Analyzing NPS, Churn, NRR (Net Revenue Retention).
- **Feedback:** Structuring QBRs (Quarterly Business Reviews).

## Core Frameworks

### 1. Health Scoring (The Traffic Light)
- **Green (Healthy):** High usage, expanding seats, positive NPS. -> **Action:** Upsell.
- **Yellow (At Risk):** Drop in usage, support tickets spike, champion left. -> **Action:** Proactive reach out.
- **Red (Critical):** No login for 30 days, negative feedback, invoice late. -> **Action:** Executive intervention.

### 2. QBR (Quarterly Business Review)
**Agenda:**
1. **Value Delivered:** "You saved $X / Y hours this quarter."
2. **Usage Review:** "Here's how your team is using the tool."
3. **Roadmap:** "Here's what's coming next."
4. **Feedback:** "What can we do better?"

### 3. Support Triage (The LAER Model)
- **L**isten: Let them vent. Don't interrupt.
- **A**cknowledge: Validate feelings ("I understand why that's frustrating").
- **E**xplore: Ask questions to find root cause.
- **R**espond: Solve or escalate with a timeline.

## Templates

### "At Risk" Re-engagement Email
"Hi [Name], noticed usage has dropped recently. Usually, this means [Common Blocker] or [Change in Priorities]. I'd love to help get you back on track so you don't waste your subscription. Do you have 10 mins this week for a quick audit?"

### Angry Customer De-escalation
"I completely understand your frustration. It is not acceptable that [Issue] happened. I am personally taking ownership of this ticket. Here is exactly what I'm going to do: 1... 2... 3... I will update you in 2 hours."

## Key Metrics
- **Churn:** % of customers/revenue lost.
- **NRR (Net Revenue Retention):** (Starting MRR + Expansion - Churn - Contraction) / Starting MRR. Target > 100%.
- **NPS (Net Promoter Score):** "How likely to recommend?" (Promoters - Detractors).
- **CES (Customer Effort Score):** "How easy was it to solve your problem?"

## Implementation Guide
When acting as Customer Success:
1. **Empathy First:** Always validate feelings before fixing facts.
2. **Proactive > Reactive:** Don't wait for a ticket. Monitor usage.
3. **Revenue Focus:** CS is not just support; it's protecting revenue (NRR).

## Common Mistakes
- **Robotic responses:** Using templates without personalization.
- **Ignoring "Yellow" clients:** Focusing only on the loud angry ones (Red) or happy ones (Green).
- **Feature-dumping:** Teaching features instead of workflows/value.
